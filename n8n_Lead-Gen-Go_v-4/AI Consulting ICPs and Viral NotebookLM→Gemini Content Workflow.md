# AI Consulting ICPs and Viral NotebookLM→Gemini Content Workflow
## Overview
This report identifies high-potential ideal customer profiles (ICPs) for AI consulting and automation services, highlights current video resources that explain AI automation value for small businesses, and designs a "mechanism"-style content workflow: use NotebookLM to generate a slide deck and Gemini/Google Vids to turn that into viral, authentic, tech-focused social content.[^1][^2]
## Validation of Existing Workflow Concept
The user’s idea is to:
- Use NotebookLM to assemble structured knowledge and generate a slideshow about an AI consulting/automation offer.
- Export that material into Gemini/Google Slides/Google Vids to generate a narrated, social-ready video.

This approach matches how creators and educators are already chaining NotebookLM and Gemini: NotebookLM (or Gemini) used to generate structured content and then Gemini or Google Vids converts that into Slides and video with voiceover and music. YouTube tutorials show people using NotebookLM prompts to get better slide decks, then exporting PDFs into Gemini to refine, and Google’s own documentation describes converting Slides into videos with Gemini-generated scripts and voice-over.[^3][^4][^5][^6]

The main improvement is to explicitly embed social-first hooks, pain points, and short-form scripting patterns into the prompts so the outputs are not generic slide decks but assets optimized for TikTok, Reels, and YouTube Shorts virality.[^7][^8]

## ICPs for AI Consulting & Automation (Small Biz / SMB)
Industry sources note that a large share of small firms are interested in AI but lack clear adoption paths: one study cited in an AI consulting report notes 79% of small firms want to learn how to make AI work for their business and roughly one in four already use AI to stay competitive. Videos aimed at AI consulting agencies highlight repeatable success in local services, healthcare, real estate, and e-commerce verticals.[^1][^2][^11]
### Core ICP Archetypes (5–10)
Below are practical ICPs that both need and tend to want AI automation, distilled from case examples and market commentary:[^1][^2]

1. **Local Professional Services Clinics** (dental, med spa, physiotherapy)
   - Pain: missed calls, manual intake, appointment juggling, follow-up gaps.
   - AI fit: automated intake forms, SMS/email reminders, triage chatbots, review requests.

2. **Residential Real Estate and Mortgage Teams**
   - Pain: slow lead response, inconsistent follow-up, manual pipeline tracking.
   - AI fit: instant lead-response agents, automated nurture sequences, deal-stage summaries, listing description generation.[^1]

3. **Boutique E‑commerce Brands (Shopify/WooCommerce)**
   - Pain: inventory forecasting, abandoned cart recovery, support tickets, ad copy.
   - AI fit: demand forecasting, AI helpdesk, dynamic email flows, product description and UGC post generation.[^2][^1]

4. **Local Home Services Companies** (HVAC, roofing, cleaning, landscaping)
   - Pain: scheduling chaos, quoting, follow-ups, review generation.
   - AI fit: conversational booking, automated estimates from intake forms/photos, follow-up/review flows.

5. **Small Law / Accounting / Bookkeeping Firms**
   - Pain: repetitive document drafting, intake, document review, basic client updates.
   - AI fit: document templates, summarization, client FAQ bots, routine update emails with human approval.

6. **Niche Agencies and Coaches** (marketing agencies, creators, consultants)
   - Pain: content volume, reporting, campaign iteration, funnel operations.
   - AI fit: content batching, analytics summaries, campaign testing workflows, SOP-driven automation across tools.[^11]

7. **Multi-location Clinics / Franchises**
   - Pain: inconsistent processes across locations, fragmented data, variable customer experience.
   - AI fit: standardized playbooks plus AI assistants for staff, central reporting, automated QA across locations.[^2]

8. **Education / Training Businesses (online course creators, local academies)**
   - Pain: content creation, student comms, enrollment funnels, certification reminders.
   - AI fit: curriculum mapping, automated email sequences, FAQ/chat support, grading or feedback helpers.

Each ICP is suitable for a “done-with-you then productized” model: start with a paid audit, ship one or two fast-win automations, then move to a monthly optimization package.
## NotebookLM "Mechanism" Prompt (Slide Deck Generator)
NotebookLM works best when provided with source docs and a clear prompt for structure and tone; practitioners recommend first asking for an outline, refining it, then having the tool write slides to match. The following prompt assumes you upload your own positioning notes and maybe transcripts or summaries from several of the videos above.[^3][^13]
### Prompt: AI Consulting Mechanism Slide Deck (NotebookLM)
> You are helping me design a slide deck for a small-business AI consulting and automation offer.
>
> Use the sources in this notebook (business notes, case studies, and AI consulting videos) to create a concise, story-driven presentation.
>
> GOAL
> - Explain "our mechanism" clearly to non-technical small business owners.
> - Show how AI consulting + automation improves profit, time, and customer experience.
> - Make it easy for them to see themselves in the examples and book a call.
>
> AUDIENCE
> - Owner or operator of a 3–50 person business (services, clinics, real estate, agencies, e‑commerce).
> - They’ve heard about AI but are overwhelmed and worried about wasting money.
>
> OUR MECHANISM (FRAME IT THIS WAY)
> - Step 1 – Audit: map current workflows, tools, and bottlenecks.
> - Step 2 – Automate: implement 1–3 simple, high-ROI AI workflows (no-code/low-code, fast wins).
> - Step 3 – Optimize: track metrics and refine over 30–90 days for compounding results.
>
> TASKS
> 1. Propose a 12–15 slide outline using this structure:
>    - Hook (pain + future state)
>    - Credibility / who we are
>    - What’s broken today (3–5 common problems)
>    - The 3-step mechanism (Audit → Automate → Optimize)
>    - 3–5 quick case-style examples (real estate, clinic, e‑com, agency, local services)
>    - ROI snapshot (time saved, leads recovered, fewer mistakes)
>    - "Why now" (AI adoption urgency)
>    - Offer & pricing (15% below typical agency retainers; first 25 partners get 25% off)
>    - Next step (book a free workflow audit)
> 2. After proposing the outline, wait.
> 3. When I say "GO", generate slide content:
>    - For each slide: a short title, 3–5 bullet points, and 1–2 suggested visuals.
>    - Use plain, direct language, not corporate jargon.
>    - Call out where a chart, screenshot, or before/after visual would help.
>
> STYLE
> - Tone: calm, confident, no hype, focused on numbers and real workflows.
> - Assume slides will later be adapted into short social videos (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts), so make slide titles strong hooks.
> - Avoid technical acronyms unless they’re explained.
>
> OUTPUT
> - First: only the slide outline (number + title + 1–2 bullet summary).
> - Second (after I approve): full slide content in a copy-pastable format for Google Slides.

This prompt embeds the "mechanism" and pricing hooks while setting up the deck to be repurposed into short-form content.
## Gemini / Google Vids Video Prompt (from Slides)
Gemini can take a deck or PDF and generate a video script, images, and voiceover, and Google Vids can convert Slides into an AI-narrated video. Viral content best practices for TikTok/short-form emphasize strong hooks in the first few seconds, short scenes, trend-aware sounds, and authenticity over over-produced polish.[^4][^7][^5][^14][^8]

Assume the NotebookLM-generated deck is in Google Slides.
### Prompt: Turn Slides into Viral Tech Social Video (Gemini / Vids)
> You are an AI video producer and social media strategist.
>
> I’ve uploaded a Google Slides deck about our small-business AI consulting + automation offer.
>
> GOAL
> - Create a 60–90 second vertical video (TikTok, Reels, Shorts) that feels like a modern tech creator, not a corporate ad.
> - Target: small business owners (3–50 employees) who are curious about AI but overwhelmed.
> - Objective: get them to click through to book a free workflow audit.
>
> CONSTRAINTS
> - Use only the information in the slides plus your general knowledge of small business AI automation.
> - Prioritize clarity and authenticity over hype.
> - Assume this will be used with AI voiceover and B‑roll, not a talking head.
>
> STRUCTURE
> 1. Hook (first 3–5 seconds):
>    - Present a painful, specific situation (e.g., "You’re losing leads every weekend and don’t even know it") or
>    - A bold promise framed realistically (e.g., "Small clinics adding an extra 10–20 appointments a month without hiring").
> 2. Problem (10–20 seconds):
>    - Call out 2–3 common bottlenecks from the slides (missed leads, manual admin, slow follow-up, chaotic reporting).
> 3. Mechanism (20–40 seconds):
>    - Explain our 3-step mechanism in simple language (Audit → Automate → Optimize) with 1–2 concrete examples.
> 4. Social proof / outcomes (10–20 seconds):
>    - Use 2–3 quick case-style snapshots with numbers (e.g., "Real estate team cut response time from hours to seconds").
> 5. Offer & CTA (last 10–15 seconds):
>    - Clearly state pricing positioning:
>      - "We price about 15% below comparable agencies so small teams can afford AI." 
>      - "First 25 partners get 25% off onboarding." 
>    - Clear next step: "Comment ‘AI’ or tap the link in bio to book your free workflow audit."
>
> TASKS
> - Write:
>   1) A full voiceover script (60–90 seconds) broken into numbered scenes.
>   2) For each scene: suggested on-screen text (max 8–12 words), visual direction (B‑roll or simple animations), and any recommended sound/mood.
>   3) 5 hook variations for the first line so we can A/B test intros.
>   4) 10–15 hashtags targeting AI automation, small business, and operations (US market, English).
>
> STYLE
> - Match the tone and pacing of modern, educational tech creators: fast but clear, no jargon walls, no "corporate stock" language.
> - Keep scenes short and punchy to maximize retention and completion rate.
> - Emphasize time saved, mistakes reduced, and new revenue unlocked, not just "AI is cool."
>
> OUTPUT FORMAT
> - Use a clear markdown structure:
>   - **Script**
>   - **Scene Breakdown**
>   - **Hook Variations**
>   - **Hashtags**

This prompt bakes in viral content patterns (strong hooks, short scenes, testing multiple intros, hashtags) while staying authentic and value-driven.
## Pricing and Positioning Elements
AI consulting content aimed at small businesses often emphasizes fast wins, clear ROI, and accessible pricing rather than complex retainers; creators and agencies focus on "quick wins first, then scale" and budget sensitivity for small firms.[^1][^2][^11]

For your offer, the report’s prompts integrate:
- **15% lower price point than comparable agencies**: communicated in the Slides (offer section) and repeated in the video script as "about 15% below comparable agencies".
- **First 25 partners/clients get 25% off**: framed as an early-partner incentive and scarcity-based hook in both deck and video CTA.

These elements position the service as premium but accessible, while giving a simple, memorable reason to act now (partner discount for first 25 clients).
## Additional Suggestions
1. **Create ICP-specific variants**: For each of 3–4 ICPs (e.g., clinic, real estate, e‑commerce, agency), spin slight variations of the NotebookLM/Gemini prompts focusing on one niche’s workflows and case examples.
2. **Leverage comment-to-content loops**: Viral content guides emphasize replying to comments with new videos and using questions as future hooks. Include in your CTA: "Comment your business type and I’ll reply with one AI workflow you could automate this month."[^7][^8]
3. **Track and iterate**: Use a simple Notion/Sheet to track which hooks, ICP angles, and offers perform best (views, watch time, click-throughs, booked audits). Then refine prompts and deck content over time.
4. **Bundle audits + implementation**: Mirror the consulting patterns from reference videos by structuring offers as: paid audit → 1–3 fast automations → monthly optimization, which lends itself well to both Slides and video narratives.[^1][^2]
