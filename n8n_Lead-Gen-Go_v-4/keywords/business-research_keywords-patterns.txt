3) Cold email best practices you should bake in
Aim for 50–125 words per email; shorter emails correlate with higher replies.​

Personalize to the problem (speed, ADA, HIPAA), not just “[FIRSTNAME].”​

One clear, low-friction CTA per email (e.g., “Open to a 15‑minute call?” or “Should I send the report?”).​

Use 1–2 contacts per company; blasting 10+ hurts response rates.​

Small, targeted campaigns (<100 recipients) get the best reply rates.​

1–2 follow-ups are usually enough; long sequences can reduce replies.​

Your funnel is perfect for this: your pre‑audit and CWV/Lighthouse + AI scoring give you something concrete to show, which is the exact “value first” that improves cold email outcomes.​

4) Free / high-ROI platforms you should focus on
Best for a solo B2B technical service in 2025:

LinkedIn

Why: ~80% of B2B social media leads come from LinkedIn; it’s the top B2B platform.​

Use: Personal profile + company page; post short “before/after” web performance stories; DM warm leads from your email list.

Email (cold outbound)

Not a “platform” brand, but the main conversion engine for B2B. Benchmarks and studies focus on email for lead generation.​

Use: Your CSV → segmented campaigns → tight CTAs → free audit offer.

Reddit (niche subs)

Why: B2B discussions and tech-savvy SMBs hang out, but you must lead with value and be subtle.​

Use: Participate in webdev, small business, startup, marketing subs. Share high-signal audits (anonymized) and answer questions.

YouTube (short educational)

Why: Strong B2B content channel; can show “live audits” and build authority.​

Use: 5–10 min “I audited this local site; here are the 3 problems” videos.

X/Twitter

Good for dev/community presence, less for pure lead conversion, but still helps with authority if you keep it concise and technical.​
