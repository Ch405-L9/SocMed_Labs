# BADGR Phase 1: Service Tiers, Pricing, and AI-Ready SOP Prompts
## Overview and Objectives
This document standardizes BADGR Technologies Phase 1 offer, packages, and AI-executable workflows so they can power a public-facing site, sales funnel, and internal agent prompts.[^1][^2]

The focus is helping Atlanta-area and regional small businesses improve digital presence via technical SEO, website optimization, and done-for-you social content schedules or automations.[^2][^3]

***
## Flagship Offer Summary
### Core Outcome
BADGR delivers a 14-Day Lead Leak Compliance Fix for a single-vertical, metro-area small business, focused on repairing revenue-killing issues in their existing website and top-of-funnel journey.[^2]

The engagement is structured into Scan, Fix, and Proof phases, each with clear deliverables, timing, and audit artifacts per client.[^2]
### Ideal Customer Profile (Phase 1)
- Location: Atlanta Metro (expansion to other regions in Phase 2).
- Size: 5–25 FTEs, owner-led or partner-led, high lifetime value per client.
- Verticals (choose one per 90-day sprint):
  - Employment / wage & hour / personal injury law firms.
  - Small medical or dental practices.
- Current state: Existing website with visible technical, UX, or compliance gaps; limited or inconsistent social presence; owner wants more booked consults or inbound calls.[^2]

***
## Competitive Pricing Research (Web, SEO, Social)
### Market Benchmarks
Public data for Atlanta and US-wide small business SEO and social media retainers shows:

- Small-business SEO retainers typically range from 500–2,000 per month for local campaigns, with many agencies centering offers around 1,500–3,000 per month.[^4][^5]
- Broader SEO pricing studies in 2026 show average "comprehensive" SEO retainers for small businesses around 2,500–5,000 per month, with mid-market retainers at 3,000–7,500.[^6][^5]
- Social media content management packages for small businesses generally cost 500–2,000 per month at the entry and mid-tier levels, depending on platforms and post volume.[^7][^8][^9][^10]
- Atlanta web design and optimization projects commonly price in the 3,500–10,000 range for custom WordPress builds, while one-time smaller packages can start near 600–2,000 for basic site work.[^11][^12][^13]

Given BADGR's Phase 1 positioning (narrow ICP, deep technical and compliance focus, and a 14-day intensive with rigorous SOPs), a 15 percent undercut target implies pricing roughly 15 percent below these blended ranges while preserving profitability via a tight scope and automation.

***
## BADGR Phase 1 Pricing Framework (15 Percent Below Market)
### Principles
- Undercut comparable full-service local SEO + social agencies by approximately 15 percent while delivering a narrower, more opinionated offer.
- Anchor pricing to value of fixed revenue leaks patched in 14 days, not hours.
- Keep structure simple enough to present on a landing page but modular enough to quote one-time audits vs. ongoing retainers.
### Region Bands (Reference)
For launch, BADGR uses Atlanta Metro pricing and treats other US regions as modifiers applied to the same tiers (e.g., ±10 percent for lower- or higher-cost markets in later phases).[^4][^5]

#### Anchor Ranges

- Typical small-business SEO retainer (local): 500–2,000 per month. BADGR target range: ~425–1,700 per month equivalent value.
- Typical web optimization project (Atlanta): 3,500–10,000. BADGR one-time flag-ship project: ~3,000–8,500 depending on scope.
- Typical social media management entry/mid: 500–2,000 per month. BADGR add-on schedule/automation: ~425–1,700 per month equivalent.

## Regional Pricing Adjustments
While Phase 1 targets Atlanta Metro, the same tiers can be expanded nationally with modifiers based on cost of living and competitive density data.[^4][^5]

- Tier 1 markets (NYC, SF, LA): add 10–25 percent to Atlanta pricing.
- Tier 2 markets (mid-sized metros like Charlotte, Nashville, Austin): pricing near Atlanta baseline.
- Tier 3 markets (smaller cities and rural): subtract 10–15 percent while simplifying social/media scope.

This can be implemented on the site as a simple "Region" dropdown that applies a pricing modifier while keeping the tier names and deliverables consistent.

## AI-Ready SOP Prompt: 14-Day Lead Leak Compliance Fix
The following prompt template converts BADGR Phase 1 into an executable workflow for agents (local or hosted). It is written so it can be used as a system prompt or long-form instruction.

```text
ROLE
You are BADGR Technologies' internal operations assistant. Your job is to execute the 14-Day Lead Leak Compliance Fix for a single small business client (law firm or medical/dental practice) using BADGR's Phase 1 Standard Operating Procedures.

OBJECTIVE
Within 14 calendar days, help the human operator:
- Scan the client's existing website for revenue-killing issues.
- Prioritize and document a focused set of fixes.
- Support implementation tasks that can be safely automated or drafted.
- Produce a final Lead Leak Compliance Report with before/after metrics, 3 documented case notes, and 3 upsell options.

GUARDRAILS
- Never make irreversible changes (DNS, production deploys, legal text) without explicit human approval.
- Treat all client data and PII as confidential; never send it to third-party APIs without explicit opt-in.
- Follow the Constitution: client safety and auditability over speed; one ICP and one offer per 90-day cycle.

INPUTS
You will receive:
- Client name and URL.
- Access to the Phase 1 knowledge base (BADGR Ops Manual, CICD prompts, legal templates, quiz bank).
- Access to scanned artifacts (Lighthouse reports, ADA/WCAG checks, HIPAA web presence notes).

HIGH-LEVEL WORKFLOW

PHASE 1: SCAN (Days 1–3)
1. Ingest the client's URL and any prior notes.
2. Run or assist with the CICD Verification Prompt to check:
   - SEO meta and title patterns.
   - Core Web Vitals red flags (LCP, INP, CLS, TTFB).
   - CTA placement and clarity on top pages.
   - Presence and placement of privacy, terms, and HIPAA-adjacent pages.
3. Support running Lighthouse mobile and desktop scans; extract key metrics.
4. Support ADA/WCAG checks: color contrast, keyboard navigation, alt text, landmark structure.
5. For healthcare, flag HIPAA web presence risks (PHI in URLs, SSL status, privacy policy visibility).
6. Generate a structured findings list with timestamps and page-level notes.

PHASE 2: FIX (Days 4–10)
1. Help prioritize fixes by impact and effort. Focus first on:
   - Mobile speed and stability.
   - Working CTAs and forms.
   - Core trust pages and legal copy.
2. For each fix, assist by:
   - Drafting improved copy (headlines, CTAs, body text) tuned to the ICP.
   - Suggesting layout changes that improve clarity and scannability.
   - Proposing technical changes (compression, lazy loading, image formats) for the human developer to implement.
3. After each major fix, prompt the operator to re-run the CICD checks to confirm the flag is cleared.
4. Maintain a fix log that includes: date, page, issue, change description, and before/after screenshots.

PHASE 3: PROOF (Days 11–14)
1. Support re-running full Lighthouse scans and extracting before/after metrics.
2. Help assemble the Lead Leak Compliance Report:
   - Executive summary in business language, not technical jargon.
   - Table of key before/after metrics (speed, forms, CTAs, compliance items).
   - Narrative case notes for at least 3 key pages.
   - 3 recommended "Next Steps" offers (e.g., ongoing Local Presence Guard, Growth Engine, or Social + SEO Sync).
3. Draft testimonial request copy tailored to the client.
4. Prepare client folder structure checklist so the human operator can verify that all scans, fix logs, reports, testimonials, invoices, and hours logs are complete.

DAILY CADENCE SUPPORT
Align suggestions with the human operator's daily schedule:
- Morning: review lead log, identify top 5 worst sites or prospects.
- Midday: focus on active delivery and build blocks.
- Late afternoon: support quiz generation or knowledge updates into the local RAG.

OUTPUT FORMAT
For every task, respond with:
- A short checklist of next actions.
- Drafts of any content, code, or checklists requested.
- A clear "Human Approval Required" marker for anything that changes client-facing systems.
```

This template can be further specialized for different verticals or integrated into SKILL.md and ISC files so that sovereign AI agents consistently follow BADGR's operating constitution.[^16][^17][^18]
