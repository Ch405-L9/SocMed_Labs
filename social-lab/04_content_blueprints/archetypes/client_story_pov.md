# client_story_pov

## Series: Tech Myth Clinic / Compliance Check in 60 Seconds

**Purpose:** Bust common tech myths held by non-technical ATL business owners. Fast, credible, save-optimized. Every video should make the viewer feel smarter and want to share it with someone in their industry.

**ICP:** Non-technical owners — attorneys, practice managers, office administrators — who have heard something about tech/compliance/AI and aren't sure if it's true. The "I've been meaning to ask someone about this" audience.

**Best Platforms:**
- LinkedIn (primary — myth-busting authority content works well with B2B professionals)
- Instagram Reels (primary — share-optimized; viewers send this to colleagues)
- TikTok (secondary — good for debunking AI myths to a younger adjacent audience)
- YouTube Shorts (secondary — search-optimized for "HIPAA website" and similar queries)

**Ideal Length:** 30–50 seconds

**Visual Style:**
- On-screen text-heavy (myth statement appears first, then gets crossed out or corrected)
- Optional: split-screen "MYTH" vs "REALITY" label overlay
- Official source screenshot (HHS.gov, Google, WCAG docs) adds credibility
- Can be faceless — text + screen, no camera needed

**Hook Patterns:**
- State the myth directly: "MYTH: 'We can't have contact forms because of HIPAA.'"
- Lead with the emotion: "This misconception is costing medical practices clients every day."
- Use contrast: "Your IT vendor told you X. Here's what the actual guidance says."

**Required Assets:**
- Official source screenshots (HHS.gov, Google Search Console, PageSpeed Insights, WCAG 2.1 docs)
- BADGR checklist template (to show the "corrected" version)
- On-screen text overlays — "MYTH" in red, "REALITY" in BADGR blue (#02068D)
- Optional: quick Loom or screen recording of the actual tool/guidance page

**CTA Variants:**
- "Save this and share it with your practice manager."
- "Comment 'TRUE' or 'FALSE' — let me know what you've heard."
- "Follow — one myth debunked per week."
- "Link in bio — free compliance checklist for ATL medical practices."
