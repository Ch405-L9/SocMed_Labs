## Patterns Observed

**Format**
- Faceless screen recordings dominate tutorials — ~70% of viral tech videos
- Face-cam optional; adds trust but not required for engagement
- AI-generated content works on X/Twitter (cyberpunk/futuristic aesthetic); less proven elsewhere

**Length**
- TikTok/Reels sweet spot: 15–30 seconds
- YouTube Shorts sweet spot: 50–60 seconds
- Longer formats (45–60s) perform better when the hook is strong and pacing is tight

**Hook**
- First 3 seconds determine 80%+ of performance
- Best hooks: mid-sentence start, bold on-screen text, or a counter-intuitive claim
- Example: "…and this is why your site never makes it past page one" (mid-sentence cold open)

**Audio**
- Neutral electronic music (no lyrics) used ~90% of the time
- Trending audio gives 2–5x boost; swap in platform-trending tracks at posting time
- Silence + on-screen text also works for compliance/legal content (feels more credible)

**Engagement Mechanics**
- Saves and shares drive the algorithm harder than likes
- Tutorial content optimized for saves ("save this for later")
- Compliance/checklist content drives shares ("send this to your attorney")

**Posting Habits**
- 3–5x/week is the baseline for growth
- Optimal posting: 4:30–5:00 PM EST (post-shutdown ritual aligns perfectly)
- Consistency > volume; algorithm rewards accounts that post on a reliable schedule

**Growth Trajectory**
- Viral hits drive 10–20% follower increase per video
- Saves/shares-heavy posts compound over time (resurface in search and Explore)
- First 20 posts establish baseline; experiment log starts informing decisions after that

## Applicability to BADGR Content

BADGR's best formats:
1. **Faceless screen-share** — Lighthouse before/after, DevTools fix, form submission test
2. **Checklist teardown** — HIPAA item, ADA item, CTA gap — one per video
3. **Mid-sentence cold open** — start in the middle of a finding, not an intro
4. **Save-optimized** — frame every video as something worth coming back to ("save this before your next client meeting")
