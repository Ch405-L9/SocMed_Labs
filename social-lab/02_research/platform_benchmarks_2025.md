# platform_benchmarks_2025

## Corrected Engagement Benchmarks (2025)

Sources: Emplifi, Social Insider, Zebracat, Rival IQ, AWISEE, Predis.ai, CreatorsJet

| Platform | Avg. Engagement Rate | Notes for Small Accounts (<10K followers) | Best For BADGR |
|----------|---------------------|------------------------------------------|----------------|
| **YouTube Shorts** | 5.91% | Highest of all platforms; education niche hits 9.5% | Primary — tutorials, compliance checks |
| **TikTok** | 2.5–7.5% | Accounts <100K get 7.5%; declines as you grow | Primary — stack breakdowns, quick hacks |
| **Instagram Reels** | 0.45–0.60% avg; 1.55–4.6% for <5K accounts | Declining YoY but small accounts still thrive | Secondary — AI tool demos, before/afters |
| **Facebook Reels** | 2.07% | Better than Instagram for reach; less competitive | Optional |
| **X/Twitter** | 0.015% median | Platform in decline for most niches; text beats video | Avoid for now |

> **Key finding:** Perplexity's original research underestimated the small-creator advantage by 3x on TikTok and missed that YouTube Shorts leads all platforms on engagement rate in 2025. BADGR should prioritize Shorts + TikTok.

## Tech/Developer Niche Specifics

- Faceless screen-recordings: 70% of successful tech tutorials
- AI-generated content: 42% of creators use AI tools; faceless AI content gets 58% retention boost in finance/tech
- YouTube education niche: 9.5% engagement rate (highest sub-category)
- Saves and shares carry 3–5x more algorithm weight than likes on Reels and TikTok
- Trending audio adds 2–5x views boost; swap in at posting time, not scripting time

## Operational Cadence Integration

Content work happens **only in defined slots; no prospecting or delivery time is sacrificed.**

| Day | Content Slot | Activity |
|-----|-------------|----------|

| Thursday | 4:30–5:00 PM | Short-form recording |

3 recordings/week → posts distributed across platforms on a rolling 2-day-ahead schedule.
