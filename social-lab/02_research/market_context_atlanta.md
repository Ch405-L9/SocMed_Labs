## Mobile Reality

- **75%** of all web interactions are now mobile
- **57%** of users won't recommend a business with a poorly designed mobile site
- Mobile conversion rates lag desktop by **40%** (2.8% vs. 4.7%)
- Mobile-optimized sites achieve **40% higher conversion rates** than non-optimized
- **62%** of top-ranking websites are mobile-optimized
- Mobile users expect **2-second load times**

Law firm and medical practice websites are disproportionately desktop-built but mobile-visited. A 6-second mobile load time is not a cosmetic issue — it's a direct intake conversion failure.

## Why the Lead Leak & Compliance Fix Offer Lands

Atlanta's professional services sector (law, healthcare, dental) has three overlapping pain points:

1. **Compliance exposure:** HIPAA (contact forms, intake pages, SSL) and ADA (accessibility) requirements that their current sites often fail. This is a liability concern, not just a marketing one.
2. **Mobile performance gap:** Most practice websites were built desktop-first, 3–7 years ago, and have never been audited on mobile.
3. **Broken intake paths:** Contact forms that don't submit on mobile, missing call buttons on mobile, no call tracking. The practice is paying for ads or SEO but the intake funnel is broken.

BADGR's 14-Day Lead Leak & Compliance Fix directly addresses all three in a single, priced, time-boxed sprint — making the value proposition concrete and the risk manageable for a small firm.

## Atlanta-Specific Context

- Metro Atlanta has a dense concentration of small law firms (2–15 attorneys) and independent medical/dental practices — both industries are web-compliance-heavy (HIPAA, ADA, state bar website rules)
- The "40% of SMBs lack professional websites" stat applies nationally; in professional services, it's more nuanced — they have sites, but 70–80% have at least one critical intake failure (BADGR's scan data from 12 sites confirms this)
- The city's growth (tech sector, corporate HQ migration, population growth) is increasing competition for new clients, making intake optimization a competitive necessity, not a nice-to-have
