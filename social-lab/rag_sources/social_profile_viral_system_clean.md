Knowledge Base

## 1. Executive Overview

Short‑form tech and full‑stack developer content on TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, X, and Facebook is driven by:

- Fast hooks (0–3 seconds).
- Clear value in under 60 seconds.
- Emotionally charged but practical tips around:
- Tech stacks.
- AI tools and automation.
- Real project and funnel breakdowns.

Video and carousel formats dominate reach and engagement:

- Reels generate substantially more reach than static posts.
- Carousels sometimes slightly outperform Reels on engagement rate when depth and saves matter.

In a 25+ viral video sample for tech/dev:

- ≈80% feature human creators.
- ≈70% of tutorials are faceless screen recordings or over‑the‑shoulder walkthroughs, especially for stack breakdowns and coding demos.

This knowledge base:

1. Defines a pattern library for viral tech/developer videos.
2. Summarizes effective formats and hooks for brands like BADGR.
3. Encodes posting and growth habits.
4. Proposes a reusable directory structure for social knowledge bases.
5. Includes a reusable meta‑prompt template for any AI.

### 3.2 Platform Distribution & Short‑Form Preferences

Directionally consistent pattern:

- **Primary viral surfaces**:
- TikTok.
- Instagram Reels.
- YouTube Shorts.
- **Secondary surfaces / amplification**:
- X.
- Facebook.

External benchmarks:

- Reels:
- ~36% more reach than carousels.
- More than double reach of static images.
- Strong non‑follower discovery.
- Carousels:
- Often highest engagement per follower (saves, deep comments).
- Shorts:
- Engagement rates >5% in some 2025 benchmarks.
- Strong subscriber conversion when videos are part of series.

Best‑practice runtimes:

- TikTok, Reels:
- 15–60 seconds; ~30 seconds sweet spot if hook lands in first 3 seconds.
- Shorts:
- 50–75 seconds recommended.
- Hook in first 3 seconds.
- Mid‑video CTA around 40 seconds.

---

### 3.3 Hook, Emotion & Audio Patterns

Dominant hook types:

- High‑stakes blunt claim:
- “Your Atlanta website is costing you clients right now.”
- Counter‑intuitive promise:
- “Stop learning frameworks. Do this instead in 2026.”
- Mid‑sentence pattern interrupt:
- “…and that is why 70 percent of businesses never hit page one.”

Guideline:

- 0–3 second **non‑negotiable hook**.
- Avoid generic greetings (“Hey guys…”).
- Create immediate:
- Tension.
- Curiosity.
- Status shock.

Emotional tones:

- Motivational (“you can do this”).
- Aspirational (future identity, “version of you that ships”).
- Curiosity‑driven (“simple trick you’re missing”).
- FOMO present but balanced by empowerment.

Audio:

- Trending or popular sounds in TikTok/Reels recommendation engines.
- ~90% of viral tech clips use neutral/upbeat electronic instrumentals with minimal lyrics:
- Do not clash with voiceover.
- Support “focus” and productivity mood.

## 4. Platform Benchmarks & Growth Dynamics

### 4.1 Instagram Reels & Feed Video

- Reels:
- ~36% more reach than carousels.
- >2× reach of single photo posts.
- Engagement per follower ~1.2% (varies by niche).
- Best for:
- Discovery.
- New follower growth.
- Carousels:
- Often slightly higher engagement rates than Reels when depth/saves matter.
- Best for:
- Frameworks.
- Multi‑step breakdowns.
- Case studies.

LinkedIn:

- Human‑written posts can see ~3.7× more engagement than obviously AI‑generated posts.
- Carousels perform well for B2B educational content.

Implication:

- Use Reels for reach + discovery.
- Use carousels for depth + authority.
- On LinkedIn, AI‑assisted but human‑edited posts.

---

### 4.2 YouTube Shorts

- Engagement:
- Some 2025 reports show >5% engagement rates for Shorts.
- Good subscriber conversion if episodes form a series.
- Best‑practice structure:
- 50–75 seconds.
- Hook in first 3 seconds.
- Mid‑video CTA around 40 seconds.
- Animated captions.
- Clear visual focal points.

Content:

- Tech education and tutorials work particularly well:
- 50–60s mini‑tutorials.
- “Build this in 60 seconds.”
- End cards that loop back to the start increase watch time.

---

### 4.3 TikTok, X, Facebook Reels

TikTok:

- Success signals:
- Completion rate.
- DM shares.
- 30–60 second runtimes.
- 1–5 targeted hashtags; over‑tagging penalized.
- Tech/dev content:
- Smaller niche vs entertainment but still effective with:
- Educational breakdowns.
- AI tool demos.
- “You are doing it wrong” corrections.

X:

- Aggregate engagement declines, especially for non‑news niches.
- Better used as:
- Distribution/relationship channel.
- Surface for AI‑generated clips tied to visually striking/controversial AI topics.
- Viral AI clips can still hit 100k+ views via quote‑retweets.

Facebook Reels:

- Useful for:
- Beginner tech tips.
- Broad mainstream audiences.
- Strategies:
- Trend‑aligned audio.
- Consistent posting.
- Strong “watch until the end” payoffs.

## 5. Viral Tech Content Pattern Library

### 5.1 Content Archetypes Table

```markdown
| Archetype | Best Platforms | Ideal Length | Visual Style | Typical Hook Example | Emotional Tone |
|-----------------------------|--------------------------|--------------|-----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Before/After Transformation | TikTok, Reels | 20–40 s | Split‑screen / flash cuts, metrics overlays | “Before this, my site took 8s to load. Now it’s under 1s.” | Satisfying, proof‑driven, awe |
| Educational 5‑Tip Breakdown | TikTok, Shorts, Reels | 30–60 s | Bullet overlays, screen‑share + talking‑head | “5 full‑stack shortcuts I wish I knew in 2018.” | Helpful, authoritative |
| AI Tool Demo (Faceless) | TikTok, Shorts | 20–45 s | Cursor/IDE capture, captions, small cam optional | “This AI tool writes 80% of my boilerplate.” | Curious, efficient |
| Behind‑the‑Build / Process | Shorts, LinkedIn, Reels | 45–75 s | Timeline, commits, quick cuts, VS Code + browser | “Here’s how this landing page went from blank file to live in 2 hours.” | Craft, mastery |
| Problem–Solution Reveal | TikTok, Reels | 20–45 s | Pain first, then fix, big captions | “Your funnel is losing 40% of leads on this one screen.” | Urgent, corrective |
| POV Client Story | Reels, LinkedIn | 45–90 s | Face cam + screens + testimonial overlay | “A local restaurant asked why online orders were flat...” | Relatable, narrative |
| Q&A / Comment Reply | TikTok, Reels | 15–30 s | Comment screenshot + quick answer | “You asked how to start with no budget; here’s my stack.” | Responsive, friendly |
```

Archetype performance (directional):

- Before/after transformations:
- Highest DM shares.
- Strong “proof” and replay.
- Educational breakdowns:
- Strong saves and replays.
- Client stories:
- High comment depth and qualified leads.

---

### 5.2 Faceless vs Face‑Forward Patterns

Approximate split (from sample):

- ~70% of viral **tutorials/stack breakdowns** are faceless:
- Clean screen recording.
- Zooms and captions.
- Over‑the‑shoulder coding.
- Face‑forward content dominates in:
- Brand storytelling.
- Founder narratives.
- Client case studies.

Implications:

- Use **faceless or minimal‑face** content for:
- Fast technical tips.
- AI demos.
- Code walkthroughs.
- Use **face‑forward** for:
- Founder narrative.
- Trust building.
- POV client stories.
- Thought leadership (especially on LinkedIn and longer YouTube).

---

### 5.3 Posting Cadence & Habit Loops

Recommended early‑stage cadence:

- 3–4 Shorts/Reels/TikToks per week:
- Batch‑filmed in one session.
- Each mapped to a pattern from the archetype table.
- 1–2 longer videos per week:
- YouTube or LinkedIn for depth and authority.
- Comment reply clips:
- Opportunistic when questions repeat.

Principles:

- Quality and experimentation > raw frequency.
- Give time to measure:
- Watch‑time.
- Saves.
- Click‑through.
- Use repeated series for:
- Learning.
- Audience familiarity.

## 7. Meta‑Prompt Template for Social/Video Research

### 7.1 Meta‑Prompt (store as `03_prompts/meta_prompt_social_lab.md`)

```markdown
# Role and Objective

You are a senior growth strategist and social-video research analyst.

Objectives:
1) Map current viral patterns for tech/developer/AI content across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, X, and Facebook.
2) Cross-check or correct any research I provide.
3) Produce a tactical content and ad system tailored to my brand configuration.

# Brand Configuration

- Brand name: {{brand_name}}
- Niche: {{niche_description}}
- Core offers: {{offers}}
- Ideal customers: {{icp_description}}
- Tone of voice: {{tone_description}}
- Visual style: {{visual_notes}}

Use this configuration for all hooks, examples, and scripts.

# Phase 1 – Research and Validation

1. Independently research 25–40 recent viral videos matching:
- Topics: full-stack development, web dev, AI tools, automation, indie SaaS, tech freelancing.
- Platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, X videos, Facebook Reels.
- Formats: short-form (15–90 seconds).

2. For each platform, synthesize:
- Typical viral view range for small/medium creators.
- Best-performing formats and hooks.
- Ideal video length and posting frequency.
- Key engagement signals (e.g., saves, shares, watch time).

3. If I provide previous research:
- Mark what is correct, outdated, incomplete, or misleading.
- Update benchmarks with newer or more authoritative sources.

**Output (Phase 1):**
- 1 table: platform benchmarks.
- 1 table: top 8–10 content archetypes.
- 10–15 bullet pattern statements.

# Phase 2 – Brand-Aligned Content System

Using Phase 1 + brand config:

1. Propose 3–5 recurring video series for {{brand_name}}.
2. For each series, specify:
- Target platform(s).
- Format (faceless vs face-forward; screen-share vs talking-head).
- Ideal length and posting cadence.
- Example 0–3 second hooks.
- Example CTAs aligned with {{offers}}.

3. Design a 4-week content calendar:
- Table with Day, Platform, Series, Hook idea, CTA.

# Phase 3 – Execution Blueprints

1. Provide 3–5 reusable script templates:
- Before/after transformation.
- 5-tip educational breakdown.
- AI tool demo (faceless).
- Client story POV.
- Q&A / comment reply.

2. Each template must include:
- Scene-by-scene structure (A-roll/B-roll/overlays).
- Voiceover outline.
- Text overlays and caption style recommendations.

3. Show how to adapt each template for:
- Organic posts.
- Paid ads linking to {{primary_funnel}}.

# Phase 4 – Knowledge Base and Metrics

1. Recommend directory layout for:
- Brand configuration.
- Research snapshots.
- Prompt templates.
- Script templates.
- Experiment logs and analytics.

2. Define 5–7 core KPIs and realistic benchmark ranges for months 1–3.

# Output Rules

- Use GitHub-Flavored Markdown.
- Use clear headings for each Phase.
- Use tables for comparisons.
- Make all recommendations specific to {{brand_name}} and {{niche_description}}.
- When data is uncertain, state assumptions explicitly.
```

## 8. KPIs & Experiment Log (Summary)

Recommended KPIs:

- Views per video.
- Average watch time and 3‑second hold rate.
- Saves and shares per 1,000 views.
- Click‑through rate to landing pages.
- Discovery vs follower reach.
- Booked calls and closed clients attributed to content.

Experiment log (CSV format):

```text
date,platform,series,script_id,hook,video_length_sec,views,avg_watch_time_sec,saves,shares,comments,clicks,booked_calls,closed_clients,notes
```

Usage:

- Append one row per published video.
- Use log as ground truth for future RAG and pattern updates.
